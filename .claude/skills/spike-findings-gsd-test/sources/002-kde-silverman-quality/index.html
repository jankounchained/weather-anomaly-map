What "error vs truth" means: normalized L1 distance between the recent-half curve and the full 30-yr pool curve (the best available estimate of the real distribution). At the real n=55 it sits ~15–17%; the floor at n_min=20 is where it crosses ~1.7× that. Note the recent curve never gets spiky as you thin it — Silverman widens its own bandwidth to compensate — it gets wrong-shaped. That's the honesty problem the rug fallback solves.
