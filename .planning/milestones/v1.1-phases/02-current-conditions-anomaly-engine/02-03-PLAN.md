## Phase Goal (recap)

**As a** visitor checking a location, **I want to** see today's temperature and how it compares to the 30-year norm for this calendar day, **so that** I can tell at a glance whether today is unusually warm or cold.

> Plan 03 is the human verification gate for the completed slice. No code changes — it confirms the built card satisfies the phase's user-facing decisions (D-06, D-07, D-08, D-09) and success criteria before the phase is marked complete.

<objective>
Verify the completed Current Conditions & Anomaly Engine phase end-to-end against a live location: current temperature (CURR-01), the delta hero (ANOM-01), the z-score badge (ANOM-02), the plain-language verdict (ANOM-03), and the statistically sound 30-year baseline behavior (ANOM-04), including the D-06/D-07/D-08/D-09 presentation decisions.

Purpose: The anomaly numbers and their visual hierarchy are the product's core value and cannot be fully proven by unit tests alone — a human must confirm the card reads correctly at a glance and behaves correctly across a couple of real locations.

Output: Human confirmation (or a punch list of issues that routes to gap closure).
</objective>

  <name>Task 1: Human-verify the full anomaly card end-to-end</name>
  <action>Run the app and walk through the how-to-verify steps below against two real locations, confirming the current temperature, delta hero, verdict, z-score badge, tooltip, combined loading, and precision decisions (D-06/D-07/D-08/D-09). This is a manual verification gate — no source files are modified.</action>
  <files>(none — verification only)</files>
  <what-built>
    The full anomaly card: a pinned location fetches today's temperature (Open-Meteo forecast) and a 30-year day-of-year baseline (Open-Meteo archive, ±5-day window, sample stddev), then displays — together, after both calls resolve (D-09) — the current temperature, a hero whole-number °C delta (ANOM-01/D-06), a plain-language verdict headline (ANOM-03/D-03/D-04), a secondary z-score badge to 1 decimal (ANOM-02/D-06), and an unobtrusive data-quality info tooltip (D-07). The delta is visually dominant over the z-score (D-08).
  </what-built>
  <how-to-verify>
    1. Start the dev server: `npm run dev` and open the printed local URL.
    2. Click the map to drop a pin on a mid-latitude land location (e.g. a European or North American city). Confirm:
       - A single combined loading state shows briefly, then the current temperature, delta, verdict, and z-score all appear together — the current temperature must NOT pop in before the anomaly finishes (D-09).
       - The delta is a whole number with a sign and °C (e.g. "+3°C"), rendered large/hero-sized (D-06, D-08, ANOM-01).
       - The verdict headline sits directly beneath the delta and reads in neutral tone as one of: Much colder / Slightly colder / Typical for today / Slightly warmer / Much warmer than usual (D-03, D-04, ANOM-03).
       - The z-score appears as a small secondary badge/chip to the side, to 1 decimal place (e.g. "z 0.8"), clearly less prominent than the delta (D-06, D-08, ANOM-02).
       - Hovering/tapping the info affordance shows: "Based on modeled climate data for this area (~9-25km resolution)" (D-07).
    3. Drop a second pin somewhere far away (different climate/timezone, e.g. an Australian or Pacific city) and confirm the temperature and anomaly update, and "today" reflects the pin's local date (not your browser's).
    4. Confirm nothing in the card ever reads "NaN", "Infinity", or "undefined" (Pitfall 2 / V5 defensive parsing).
    5. Optional sanity: reload the page on a shared URL that already carries lat/lng and confirm the card resolves without visibly flashing the default map center.
  </how-to-verify>
